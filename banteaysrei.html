Banteay Srei – The Jewel of Khmer Art

Banteay Srei is often called the crown jewel of Khmer architecture. Although smaller than Angkor Wat or Bayon, its beauty lies in extraordinary craftsmanship.

Built in the 10th century from rare pink sandstone, this temple is famous for carvings so detailed that even after more than a thousand years, floral patterns, mythological scenes, and divine figures remain astonishingly sharp.

Dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva, Banteay Srei represents artistic perfection rather than monumental scale. Every corner of the temple feels carefully sculpted, elegant, and intimate. Many travelers consider it the most beautiful temple in Cambodia.
