Phnom Kulen – Birthplace of the Khmer Empire

Phnom Kulen is one of Cambodia’s holiest mountains and is widely considered the birthplace of the Khmer Empire.

In 802 AD, King Jayavarman II proclaimed independence here and declared himself the universal monarch, laying the foundation for one of Southeast Asia’s greatest civilizations.

Today, Phnom Kulen is famous for waterfalls, sacred carvings, and religious pilgrimage sites, combining natural beauty with historical significance.
