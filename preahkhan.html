Preah Khan – The Sacred Sword

Preah Khan was built by Jayavarman VII to honor his father and served as a temple, university, and administrative center all at once.

Historical records suggest nearly 100,000 people were connected to its operation, including monks, dancers, scholars, and royal officials.

Its endless corridors, partially collapsed chambers, and overgrown courtyards create the feeling of exploring a forgotten city hidden beneath layers of time.
