Kbal Spean – The River of a Thousand Lingas

Kbal Spean is one of Cambodia’s most unusual sacred sites. Instead of temple towers, its artistry is carved directly into the riverbed.

Hundreds of lingas, lotus patterns, and Hindu deities were etched into stone so flowing water would symbolically bless Angkor below.

Reaching the site requires a forest hike, making the journey itself feel adventurous and rewarding. Hidden among jungle sounds and flowing water, Kbal Spean feels like a secret chapter of Khmer spirituality.
