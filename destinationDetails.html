UNESCO World Heritage Khmer Architecture Must See

About This Destination

Angkor Wat is the largest religious monument in the world, built in the early 12th century by King Suryavarman II. Originally a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, it gradually became a Theravada Buddhist temple. Its five iconic lotus towers represent the peaks of Mount Meru, the home of the gods in Hindu cosmology.

The complex is surrounded by a vast 190-metre wide moat and features over 800 metres of intricate bas-relief galleries — among the largest and most exquisite examples of classical Khmer art in existence. The temple covers 162.6 hectares, and the outer wall alone stretches 1,025 metres by 800 metres. Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1992, it remains Cambodia's most iconic landmark and the symbol on its national flag.

Top Highlights

✦

Sunrise over the Lotus Ponds

Arrive before 5 AM for the golden reflection of the towers in the reflection pools — one of the most photographed moments in Southeast Asia.

✦

Bas-Relief Gallery — Churning of the Ocean of Milk

A 49-metre panel depicting one of Hinduism's greatest creation myths, carved in extraordinary detail across the eastern gallery.

✦

Climb to the Central Tower

The steep climb to the upper terrace rewards with panoramic views across the surrounding jungle and causeway.

✦

Spring Equinox Solar Alignment

During the equinox, the sun rises perfectly behind the central spire — a deliberate feat of ancient astronomical engineering.

Practical Tips

Dress Code

Shoulders and knees must be covered. Sarongs are available at the entrance if needed.

Best Season

November to February offers the coolest weather and clearest skies for photography.

Getting There

10–15 minute tuk-tuk ride from central Siem Reap. Bicycle hire is also popular.

Guided Tours

Licensed guides are available at the gate and add immense context to the bas-reliefs and temple history.
