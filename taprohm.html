Ta Prohm – Where the Jungle Reclaims Civilization

Ta Prohm is one of Angkor’s most atmospheric and unforgettable temples. Constructed in 1186 by King Jayavarman VII, the temple was originally built as a Buddhist monastery and university dedicated to his mother.

Unlike many other Angkor temples that were heavily restored, Ta Prohm was intentionally left in a semi-natural state. Massive silk-cotton trees and strangler figs wrap themselves around walls, windows, and towers, making it look as though nature is slowly reclaiming the ruins.

This haunting balance between architecture and wilderness gives Ta Prohm its unique identity. Sunlight filters through broken stone corridors while roots twist across collapsed galleries. It is not simply a temple to admire—it is an experience that makes visitors reflect on time, impermanence, and the inevitable power of nature.
